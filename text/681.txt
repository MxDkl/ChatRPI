Visit




We are just two and a half hours north of New York City via a beautiful train ride up the Hudson River, an easy day trip from the Berkshires or Saratoga Springs, and across the river and just north of Albany, NY. We are also just under three hours by car from Boston or Montreal.
Located on the campus of Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute, the nation’s oldest technological research university, EMPAC overlooks Troy, a city that played a central role in the Industrial Revolution and that still preserves some of the 19th century’s best architecture.



Holiday closures
We are closed: Memorial, Independence, Labor, Thanksgiving, and day after, Christmas week, New Years, Martin Luther King, and President's Day.
Additional building hours for events and exhibitions.
Box Office
Performances: $20, $15 for seniors, faculty, and non-RPI students.
Films and special events: $10
Talks: Free
Rensselaer Students: Free admission to all events by swiping your ID card before the show and joining EMPAC + .  Advance tickets for students $6. 
518/276.3921
Evelyn’s Café
Evelyn’s is open from 11AM–2PM Wednesday for lunch.
In addition to cash, Evelyn's will accept all major credit cards, RAD, and FLEX payments.
Table Reservations
Table reservations for Evelyn's are available during regular café hours. Call or email Café Supervisor Dixa Bustamante. 518/276.2578
Address + Directions


Parking + Accessibility
Parking
The main entrance and box office are located on the EAST (campus) side of the building. For events, please park in the College avenue parking garage adjacent to Cogswell Laboratory.

Accessibility
The East campus entrance is also the accessible access to EMPAC. Please visit our Accessibility page for more detailed information. Note that parking on 8th Street requires using stairs to the third floor where there are elevators in the corridor hallway.
