When in Rome: Studying Abroad Shapes Academic Experience Regina Stracqualursi2022-11-29T11:26:42-05:00November 23, 2022| 

By Skye Day ’24, architecture student
As a former military child, I have moved around a lot and have lived in vastly different places. Having never been out of the country before, I was extremely anxious about living on another continent for four months by myself, but these fears were quickly alleviated when I arrived in Italy. Studying in Rome as part of my education at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute has been such a wonderful opportunity since it contains thousands of years of ancient history, both socially and architecturally. I’ve learned about how architecture can define and rule an entire culture or society through different styles, shapes, and defined uses.

We may come from different cultures, but we are able to bond over small things like food, the weather, the neighborhood cat, etc. By fostering these relationships, I have been able to enjoy Italian goods, such as handmade glass sculptures, genuine leather items, homemade pasta, fresh mozzarella, and more.
Although I want to spend every euro I have on the trinkets I see visiting different cities and have to hold myself back from having two to three cups of gelato each day, getting to experience Italian culture firsthand is truly a surreal and once-in-a-lifetime experience. Though I would be lying if I said I didn’t miss certain aspects of home, including my family and Chick-fil-A!
